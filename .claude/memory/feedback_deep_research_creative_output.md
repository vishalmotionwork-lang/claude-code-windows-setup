For strategy/research/positioning work, a handful of generic research agents + a templated HTML board is NOT acceptable. The user called it "very basic / didn't do any research / created shit / where is the creativity."

**The rule for next time:**
1. **Deploy a large multi-agent fan-out** (the user said "50+ agents") across genuinely DISTINCT angles — not "more of the same tools." Cover: direct + adjacent + *historical/old* competitors, international/indie, what we could market the product AS, and crucially **marketing teardowns of how recent people actually market** (e.g. heyclicky drop-after-drop, Cluely attention-hacking, Pieter Levels / Marc Lou indie launch cadences, shipmas/feature-drop mechanics, waitlist FOMO).
2. **Reason, don't dump.** Synthesis must have a strong point of view: non-obvious patterns, what's contrarian, what to actually DO, and what hype to ignore. No flat lists of obvious tools.
3. **Creative output must be genuinely designed** — a real aesthetic/creative direction (mood, type, color, layout, references), tailored to the audience (Gen-Z IG-native), not a generic card template.

**Why:** Vishal builds consumer products where positioning + marketing creativity + non-obvious competitive insight ARE the work. A shallow synthesis of well-known tools gives him nothing he didn't already know. Depth (breadth of search + reasoned POV) and design taste are the value.
