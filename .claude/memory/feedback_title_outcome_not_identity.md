When writing YouTube titles for Zeel (or any creator), the title must sell **what the viewer gets** — not who the creator is or what their credentials are. Nobody clicks because of the creator's backstory; they click because the title promises a specific outcome or curiosity payoff.

**Why:** User flagged 2026-04-23 that my candidate pool was generating titles like *"I Replaced 3 Agency Roles With AI — Rehired 2"* and *"What a Real Agency Pays for AI in 2026"* — both lead with creator identity/credential. User's direct quote: *"nobody cares about what she knows."* Title must stand on outcome-promise alone; the creator credential becomes supporting material in thumbnail, hook, and script — NOT title.

**How to apply:**
- Lead titles with the viewer's payoff (*"become expert in Claude in one video"*, *"the only Claude video you need"*)
- Creator credentials (non-tech founder, agency operator, India-based) belong in THUMBNAIL text / HOOK / BIO / script — not in the title itself
- When user has an instinct about a title direction, respect it and generate variations inside their frame — don't re-route to a completely different angle
- Validate variations against the actual title framework (~/Youtube plan/Frameworks/master/title-master-framework.md) — not just 2026-trend data
- Ship 5–10 variations when asked, with minimal commentary — no fluff
