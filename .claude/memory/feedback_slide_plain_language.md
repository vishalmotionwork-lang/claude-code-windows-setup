Slide copy on Vishal's presentations should read like spoken English, not paper text. Replace technical jargon with the concept in plain words.

**Why:** Vishal speaks the technical depth aloud during the talk — the slide is for the audience to follow visually. Words like "training objective", "internal representation", "tokens", "syntax", "causality", "confabulation", "abstract representations" should be swapped for everyday phrasings ("all it's trained to do", "mental picture", "words in a row", "cause-and-effect", "makes things up", "predict the meaning"). Confirmed 2026-05-09 on the World Models deck.

**How to apply:** When asked to "remove jargon" or "make it easy to understand" with qualifiers like "not much" / "lil more descriptive" — touch only heavy jargon, keep punchy short phrasings, don't expand into full paragraphs. Preserve technical names that ARE the concept being introduced (e.g., keep "JEPA", "AlphaZero", "Yann LeCun") — those go on slide as labels, the speaker explains them.
