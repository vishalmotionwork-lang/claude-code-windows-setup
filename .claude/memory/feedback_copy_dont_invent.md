When copying/reverse-engineering proven viral content, do NOT invent new concepts or "unique angles." Keep the EXACT same:
- Topic (don't broaden "Claude Code" to "AI Coding")
- Structure (same number of slides, same flow)
- Format (if it's a single image, don't turn it into a carousel)
- Visual style (match the original's aesthetic)

Only update the INFORMATION INSIDE — newer skills, better tools, fresher data, latest trends.

**Why:** User said the "unique angles" I came up with were "literal shit." The original concepts are proven to work — the framework IS the value. Changing the concept defeats the purpose of copying what works.

**How to apply:** Think "cover song" — same melody, your voice. Same skeleton, fresh meat. If the original says "5 levels of Claude Code," yours says "5 levels of Claude Code" with updated skills, NOT "5 levels of AI content creation."
