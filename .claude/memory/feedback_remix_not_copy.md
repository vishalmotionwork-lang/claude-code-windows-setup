When reverse-engineering viral content, there are THREE layers:
1. **Framework** (keep this): the structure, slide count, visual format, CTA mechanics, psychological triggers (gamification, curiosity gap, identity, etc.)
2. **Topic** (change this): the actual subject matter — must be genuinely different, not a word-swap
3. **Info** (update this): the data, examples, stats inside

Previous feedback said "keep the skeleton, update the info." That was WRONG — it produced obvious copies.

**The correct approach**: Extract WHY the post went viral (the psychology, the emotion, the hook pattern). Apply that same psychology to a completely different topic that's authentic to the brand.

**Why:** User explicitly rejected multiple rounds of:
- Word swaps ("Claude" → "AI", "developers" → "creators")
- Generic AI content topics ("Best AI Tools for Creators", "$60/month stack")
- Outcome-reframes that were still about AI tools
- All of these were "lazy" — just shuffling the same deck

**How to apply:**
- Don't generate remix topics without first understanding the brand's unique POV
- Ask what the brand wants to be known for before proposing topics
- Study the PSYCHOLOGY of why something went viral, not just its surface topic
- The remix should be unrecognizable as a copy — someone who saw the original should NOT think "oh they copied that"
